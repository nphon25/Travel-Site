Athens

Athens is the capital of Greece and one of the world’s oldest cities, with a recorded history spanning over 3,400 years. Known as the birthplace of democracy and Western philosophy, it is home to iconic ancient landmarks such as the Acropolis, the Parthenon, and the Ancient Agora. Today, Athens blends its rich classical heritage with a vibrant modern culture, offering world-class museums, lively neighborhoods, and Mediterranean cuisine. The city serves as a major hub for history, tourism, and contemporary Greek life.

Attractions

Acropolis

The Acropolis is an ancient citadel situated on a rocky hill above Athens, renowned as a symbol of classical Greek civilization. Dating back to the 5th century BCE, it features some of the most significant architectural and historical monuments in the world, including the Parthenon, the Erechtheion, and the Temple of Athena Nike. Originally built as a religious and cultural center, the Acropolis remains a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Greece’s most visited landmarks , offering insight into ancient art, architecture, and Athenian society.

Parthenon

The Parthenon is the most iconic structure on the Acropolis of Athens and a masterpiece of classical Greek architecture. Dedicated to the goddess Athena, it was completed in the 5th century BCE during the height of the Athenian Empire. Built entirely of marble, the temple is renowned for its harmonious proportions, Doric columns, and sculptural details, including the famous Parthenon frieze. Originally serving as a temple and later as a church and mosque, the Parthenon stands as a lasting symbol of ancient Greek culture, democracy, and artistic achievement.

Ancient Agora of Athens

The Ancient Agora of Athens was the political, commercial, and social heart of the city during classical antiquity. Located northwest of the Acropolis, it served as a central gathering place where citizens met for markets, public speeches, and civic decision-making. The site features important structures such as the well-preserved Temple of Hephaestus and the reconstructed Stoa of Attalos, which now houses the Agora Museum. As a hub of daily life and democratic activity in ancient Athens, the Agora offers valuable insights into the civic and cultural foundations of Western society.

Accomodations and Dining

Miravilia Kitchen

Miravilia is a restaurant where authentic Greek cuisine meets refined dining. Found in the heart of Athens, Miravilia brings you traditional, homemade Greek dishes prepared with care, premium ingredients, and a touch of creativity.

Herodion Hotel

The Herodion is very close to the southern entrance of the Acropolis. The hotel's 90 rooms blend modern design with classical influences, creating an atmosphere unlike any other. Amenities includ a rooftop lounge and restaurant, free wi-fi, and metro access to the airport.
